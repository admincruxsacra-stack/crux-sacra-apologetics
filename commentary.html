The New Testament, Verse by Verse
with the Church Fathers

The Church Fathers — Augustine of Hippo, John Chrysostom, Jerome, Ambrose, Cyril of Alexandria, and the great teachers of the early Church — spent their lives explaining what Sacred Scripture means. This tool brings their commentary directly alongside the sacred text, verse by verse, so that the reader encounters not only the Word but the mind of the Church that received it.

The Bible text presented here is the Douay-Rheims Bible (Challoner revision, 1899) — the classic English Catholic translation made directly from the Latin Vulgate, long trusted by scholars and the faithful for its fidelity to the original sources. All patristic commentary is drawn from public domain sources and attributed to its original author and work.

To begin, select a book and chapter from the navigation below. Verses with available commentary are underlined — click any underlined verse to open the Church Fathers commentary on the right. Use the verse jump dropdown to navigate directly to commented verses, or use the arrow keys to move between them.
